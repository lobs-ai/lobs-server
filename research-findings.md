### The Two Camps PAW Escapes
Every competitor fits one of two buckets:
- **Conversation** (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot): You talk, it responds. Answer-and-respond model.
- **Automation/Busywork** (Lindy, Notion AI, Zapier): Pre-defined triggers or narrow task domains.

**PAW occupies an unclaimed third position:** personal AI execution layer — goal → structure → supervised execution → outcome tracking.

### 4 Messaging Gaps
1. **Goal decomposition** — nobody says "we turn your vague goal into a project with tasks and agents"
2. **Supervised execution** — nobody claims the middle ground between full autonomy and manual prompting
3. **Spend-to-outcome visibility** — nobody tells individuals what their AI spend *actually produced*
4. **Explicitly individual / personal** — Notion/Zapier have gone team+enterprise; Copilot is general-purpose; gap exists for "built for you, not your org"

## The 3 Uniquely Ownable Claims

### Claim 1: Goal Decomposition
> *"PAW turns your goal into a project — not just an answer."*

No competitor transforms an ambiguous goal into a structured project with tasks, agent routing, and checkpoints. ChatGPT/Claude answer. Zapier automates. PAW orchestrates from goal to execution.

### Claim 2: Spend Visibility
> *"PAW is the only personal AI system that tells you what your AI spend actually produced."*

Cost tracking + activity timeline + daily ops brief = first-class personal telemetry. No competitor offers this for individual users. With $100–250/month AI spending normalized, this is a high-resonance trust + retention story.

### Claim 3: Supervised Execution
> *"Agents do the work. You approve the decisions. That's supervised execution."*

The market is bifurcated: full autonomy ("watch it happen") vs. manual prompting ("write better prompts"). PAW owns the uncontested middle: agents that execute with structured approval gates at decision points.

## Defensible Narrative (One-Paragraph Version)

> AI assistants answer your questions. AI automation runs your triggers. Neither was built for what you actually need: to go from an ambitious goal to a shipped outcome — consistently, without manual project management, and without losing control of what matters. PAW is the execution layer that closes this gap. It turns your goals into structured projects, routes work to specialized AI agents, and surfaces key decisions back to you before high-impact actions fire. It tracks what your AI spend actually produced — in shipped tasks, not just tokens. It's not a chat interface, not an automation platform, and not a team tool. It's a personal operating layer for one person who needs to execute at a level that used to require a team.
