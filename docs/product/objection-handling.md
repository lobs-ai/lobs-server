# PAW Objection Handling Guide

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-24  
**Use case:** Sales calls, evaluator reviews, onboarding conversations, and landing-page FAQ content.

## Objection 1: “This looks too complex.”

### What they usually mean
- “I’m worried setup will take too long.”
- “I don’t want another system to learn.”
- “I’m not technical.”

### Rebuttal (short)
PAW is designed to reduce complexity, not add it. You start with one goal, and PAW turns it into a clear plan and next actions.

### Rebuttal (expanded)
Most users begin with a single workflow and get value quickly. PAW provides structure and guided execution, so you don’t have to design a process from scratch. You can adopt it gradually: one project, one use case, then expand.

### Proof points to use
- Fast first-use path (single goal → actionable plan)
- Guided workflow instead of blank-slate setup
- Incremental adoption (start small, scale later)

---

## Objection 2: “This is too expensive.”

### What they usually mean
- “I’m unsure it will pay for itself.”
- “I compare this to a single tool price, not total workflow cost.”

### Rebuttal (short)
PAW replaces execution friction across multiple tools and manual steps. The ROI comes from time saved, faster shipping, and fewer dropped priorities.

### Rebuttal (expanded)
The right comparison is not one SaaS subscription—it’s the cost of stalled work, context switching, and delayed releases. PAW helps users complete more meaningful work each week, which improves output without immediately increasing headcount.

### Proof points to use
- Weekly hours recovered from coordination overhead
- Faster cycle time from idea to shipped outcome
- Consolidation of scattered tooling/workflows

---

## Objection 3: “I don’t trust AI systems with important work.”

### What they usually mean
- “Will it make mistakes?”
- “Will I lose control?”
- “What happens to my data?”

### Rebuttal (short)
PAW is built for supervised execution. You keep control over high-impact decisions, with visibility into what’s happening and why.

### Rebuttal (expanded)
PAW is not a black box autopilot. It is an execution layer with checkpoints, traceability, and explicit review where it matters. Teams can adopt guardrails that match their risk tolerance, so automation supports judgment instead of replacing it.

### Proof points to use
- Human approval points for critical actions
- Clear activity trails and decision visibility
- Explicit data/auth controls documented for evaluators

---

## Quick Response Script (for live conversations)

1. **Acknowledge:** “That concern makes sense.”  
2. **Reframe:** “PAW is designed to solve exactly that risk.”  
3. **Substantiate:** Share one concrete proof point.  
4. **Advance:** “Want to see this in a 10-minute walkthrough with your actual workflow?”

## FAQ Snippets (Landing Page Ready)

- **Is PAW hard to set up?**  
No. Most users start with one workflow and get a clear action plan quickly.

- **How do I know it’s worth the cost?**  
Measure time saved and outcomes shipped per week. PAW is built to improve both.

- **Do I stay in control?**  
Yes. You can require review for important actions and maintain full visibility.
